What are Eigenvalues?

M ⁢ v = λ ⁢ v

Let's start with what an eigenspace is. An eigenspace is a subspace of a vector space that is invariant under the action of a matrix. Now, any vector in this eigenspace is an eigenvector. Since the eigenspace is invariant, it must be stretched by some factor λ — this is the eigenvalue. Because of linearity, the eigenvalue is well-defined; that is, it is the same for all eigenvectors in a particular eigenspace.

How to Play

At the left you see ℝ2. You can click on any point of the plane to place a vector there; this vector will be drawn, together with its image under some secret matrix. The goal is to find the eigenspaces of this matrix; if you know where it is, simply click there. A short note: getting the first eigenvalue is easy; it's the second that is hard.

Also, very rarely, you'll get a matrix without eigenvalues. Sorry! Just click “Reset” under the plane. Happy eigening!
